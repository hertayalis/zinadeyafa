Ty Murray: All-Around Rodeo Champion

Ty Murray is an American rodeo cowboy and entrepreneur with a net worth of $6 million. Born in Phoenix in 1969, he was taught rodeo fundamentals as a child by his parents. Murray was mentored by world champion cowboy Larry Mahan and became the All-Around Champion Cowboy in high school. He helped Arizona win its first National High School Rodeo Association Championship in 1987.

Murray joined the Professional Rodeo Cowboys Association at 18 and became the youngest ever PRCA All-Around Rodeo Cowboy. He won the World All-Around Rodeo Champion title six years in a row from 1989 to 1994 and again in 1998. He was the top money earner in bull riding, bareback, and saddle bronc events for those years. He also won the PRCA World Bull Riding Championship in 1993 and 1998.

Due to injuries, Murray had both of his knees reconstructed in 1995 and missed about three years of action. He has been featured in commercials for Miller Lite and has appeared in Walker, Texas Ranger and CSI. Murray was a contestant on the eighth season of Dancing with the Stars.

In 1999, Murray met singer-songwriter Jewel. The couple married in 2008, but in July 2014, Jewel announced they had split up and were planning a divorce.
