Timothe Chalamet's Rap Persona Will Live on Forever

Publish date: 2024-07-22

Acclaimed actor Timothée Chalamet has had quite an interesting past. From a rapping career to painting Xbox controllers, he's always been creative.

Hollywood's favorite star Timothée Chalamet is a man of many talents. He's only 25, but he's given impressive performances in films like Call Me By Your Name, Beautiful Boy, Little Women, and Dune. The actor is currently in the United Kingdom filming his next project, Wonka, in which he'll take on the role of the titular chocolatier in a prequel story.

Not many know this, but the actor is actually also a great singer and dancer. He attended a performing arts high school in New York, so he perfected those skills, along with his acting chops, while he was a student. Timothée even developed a rap persona in his teenage years that will most definitely haunt him forever. Let's take a look at his past as a rapper.

Article continues below advertisement

Timothée Chalamet was once a rapper known by Lil Timmy Tim.

Before he made it big as a movie star, Timothée's passion was rapping. He attended the Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School of Music & Art and Performing Arts in New York, which is where he developed his rap persona.

Timothée reflected on his rapping days — during which he went by the stage names Lil Timmy Tim and Timmy Tim — in an episode of The Graham Norton Show back in 2019. Throughout the interview, he looked uncomfortable and embarrassed, but that's what makes his fans love him even more!

The interview touched on Timothée's most famous song, the absolute bop "Statistics." While in school, Timothée wrote and performed a rap for his statistics class. He sang about how much he appreciated his teacher, Ms. Lawton, and how he'd be famous one day.

Timothée Chalamet had a YouTube account called ModdedController360.

For years, fans of the superstar claimed he had a secret YouTube account. The rumors are true, as Timothée recently confirmed that he is the owner of the ModdedController360 account on YouTube, where a pre-teen Timmy can be seen showing off his custom-painted Xbox 360 controllers.

How the fans found the channel, we don't know. But what we do know is that in an interview with Nate Hill, Timothée confirmed the account was his.

"I actually had a YouTube channel that people found. It's youtube.com/moddedcontroller360. And I used to paint modded controllers. I did three, and I spray-painted them. I would like, open them up and paint them and I charged people $10."

We want to reiterate that Timothée Chalamet really is a man of many talents. He's successfully kicked off his main gig as an actor, and as a youth, he maintained side hustles like rapping and hand-painting custom Xbox controllers. Is there anything he can't do?
